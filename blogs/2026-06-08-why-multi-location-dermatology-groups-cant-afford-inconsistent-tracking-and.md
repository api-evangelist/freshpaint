Multi-location dermatology practices face measurement challenges when acquired properties track patient demand differently across locations and service lines. The article explains how standardizing tracking definitions and creating a shared data foundation enables reliable cross-location performance comparisons and attribution to actual patient outcomes rather than surface-level metrics like form fills.
